The 4 C's of Diamonds

When the GIA (Gemological Institute Of America) rates a diamond, they use the "4 C's": Color, Clarity, Cut, and Carat. Not all diamonds are created equally, so it is important to understand what drives the value of a diamond. Below you will find information as well as our recommendations on which "C's" to weigh more for different types of jewelry pieces.


Diamond colors range from D (colorless) to Z (light yellow). Colorless diamonds allow light to pass through easily, which creates a rainbow effect of color as it sparkles. The less color a diamond has, the more valuable it is. There are also fancy colors such as pink and intense yellow which are also beautiful and considered valuable.



Important for:
Engagement Rings, Earrings
Less important for:
Bracelets, Pendants


Clarity refers to inclusions and blemishes found in the stone and graded by the GIA depending on their location, type, and extent. The clarity scale ranges from:
Flawless - no inclusions
VVS - very, very slightly included
VS - very slightly included
SI - slightly included
I - imperfect observable inclusions

Important for:
Engagement Rings
Less important for:
Earrings, Bracelets, Pendants


The cut of a diamond refers to the following: proportions, symmetry and finish. When you first get a raw diamond, you need a skilled cutter to accurately cut it to the appropriate proportions. The better the cut, the better the diamond will handle light, leading to an amazing sparkle and brilliance.



Important for:
Everything


The weight of a diamond is measured in carats. As a diamonds carat weight increases, so does its rarity and price. The carat-weight of a diamond is the easiest to measure and evaluate. However, two equal carat-weight diamonds can have very different price points if their cut, color, and clarity differ.



Important for:
Matter of preference
